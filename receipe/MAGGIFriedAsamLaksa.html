MAGGI Fried Asam Laksa

Serve: 2 Person

Cook Time: 12 Minutes

-Love to Cook, Love to Eat, and Love to Live

The laksa is one of the most distinctive Malaysian dishes, a cuisine widely enjoyed throughout the country, with various states or regions having their own unique version of laksa influenced by local taste and culture. Malaysian laksa can be divided into two main variants — the curry laksa and asam laksa, with their ingredients and flavour being the distinctive factors. Asam laksa is a flavourful dish that combines the goodness of sweet, sour, tangy, gingery, slightly acidic flavours all in a bowl of stock — your taste buds are in for a treat! Asam laksa recipes usually call for thick or thin rice noodles, together with shredded mackerel, onions, cucumber, chillies, pineapple, lettuce, mint and bunga kantan — topped with prawn paste.

Ingredient

15 g Corn Oil 5 g Shallots
50 g Prawns 50 g Squid
3 Bird's EYe Chilli MAGGI® 2-Minute Noodles Asam Laksa
50 g Carrot 10 g Kesum Leaves
100 g Cabbage

How To Cook

Step 1

Cook noodles for 1 ½ minutes.


Step 2

Drain then toss under running water till cool (to prevent noodles from getting mushy).


Step 3

Add a little oil so noodles don’t stick together


Step 4

Stir fry red onions, prawns, squid, and bird’s eye chillies.

Step 5

Add blanched MAGGI® noodles, cabbage, daun kesum, and carrots.

Step 6

Stir and add seasonings.

Step 7

Stir till fully absorbed for 1 – 2 minutes.
